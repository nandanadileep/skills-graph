I read the paper 'Attention Is All You Need' by Vaswani et al. This introduced the Transformer architecture, moving away from recurrence and convolutions to rely entirely on attention mechanisms. It's become the backbone for almost all modern large language models. My takeaway is that this simple, parallelizable architecture unlocked the scaling laws we see today.
